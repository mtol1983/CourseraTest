# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to creating a respectful, inclusive, and welcoming environment for everyone who participates in this project or community.

We pledge to make participation a positive experience for all contributors, maintainers, users, and community members, regardless of background, experience level, identity, ability, or point of view.

We expect everyone involved in this project to act with professionalism, kindness, patience, and respect.

## Our Standards

Examples of behaviour that contributes to a positive environment include:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of different opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback respectfully
- Showing empathy toward other community members
- Accepting responsibility and apologising when mistakes are made
- Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
- Helping others learn, contribute, and participate

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- Harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- Offensive, insulting, or discriminatory comments
- Trolling, deliberate disruption, or inflammatory behaviour
- Public or private harassment
- Publishing or sharing another person’s private information without permission
- Unwelcome attention or repeated unwanted contact
- Abusive, threatening, or aggressive communication
- Any behaviour that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or community setting

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that do not follow this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers are also responsible for responding to reported violations in a fair, consistent, and respectful manner.

Maintainers should protect the privacy and safety of people who report concerns, where reasonably possible.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project and community spaces, including but not limited to:

- Source code repositories
- Issue trackers
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Documentation
- Community chats
- Meetings and events
- Email communications
- Social media or public spaces where someone is representing the project

This Code of Conduct also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behaviour may be reported to the project maintainers.

Reports should include as much relevant information as possible, such as:

- What happened
- Where it happened
- When it happened
- Who was involved
- Any supporting links, screenshots, or context

All reports will be reviewed and investigated as appropriate.

Maintainers may take any action they consider necessary and appropriate, including removing content, issuing warnings, limiting participation, or banning a participant from the project or community.

Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern or participates in an investigation is not allowed.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers may use the following guidelines when deciding how to respond to violations of this Code of Conduct.

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** A minor issue or behaviour that may be unprofessional or inappropriate, but does not appear to be harmful or repeated.

**Consequence:** The participant may receive a private written warning explaining the issue and requesting that the behaviour stop.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** Behaviour that negatively affects others or disrupts the project or community.

**Consequence:** The participant may receive a formal warning. Continued behaviour may result in temporary or permanent restrictions.

### 3. Temporary Restriction

**Impact:** Serious or repeated inappropriate behaviour, harassment, disruption, or failure to follow previous warnings.

**Consequence:** The participant may be temporarily restricted from participating in the project or community for a specified period.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Impact:** Severe, harmful, threatening, abusive, discriminatory, or repeated behaviour that makes the community unsafe or unwelcoming.

**Consequence:** The participant may be permanently banned from participating in the project or community.
